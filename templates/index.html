// System Pipeline

How the Forecasting Works

01

Sensor Data Collection

Wind speed, direction, temperature, rotor speed, and atmospheric pressure are captured at 10-minute intervals from turbine sensors.

→
02

Feature Scaling

StandardScaler normalises all input features to zero mean and unit variance, ensuring equal contribution to distance calculations in the KNN model.

→
03

KNN Regression (k=5)

The K-Nearest Neighbours model identifies the 5 most similar historical observations and averages their power outputs to generate a precise forecast.

→
04

Power Output Prediction

The model outputs Active Wind Power in kilowatts (kW) instantly, with evaluation metrics MAE, RMSE, and R² confirming model reliability.

Input Features & Target Variable

Wind Speed
m/s

Primary driver of power generation. Ranges from calm to storm-force conditions.

Wind Direction
degrees (0°–360°)

Affects turbine yaw alignment and effective swept area efficiency.

Temperature
°C

Influences air density, which directly impacts aerodynamic power extraction.

Rotor Speed
RPM

Angular velocity of the turbine blades — a key mechanical efficiency indicator.

Atmospheric Pressure
hPa

Barometric pressure determines air mass density and power curve adjustments.

Active Wind Power TARGET
kW

The predicted output variable — instantaneous electrical power generated by the wind turbine.

// Motivation

Why Accurate Forecasting Matters

Wind energy is inherently variable — gusts, pressure fronts, and temperature shifts can cause power output to fluctuate by hundreds of kilowatts within minutes. Without accurate forecasting, grid operators must maintain expensive spinning reserves.

Our ML-based system uses historical patterns from 50,000 data points spanning a full year of turbine operation to deliver predictions that reduce uncertainty and enable proactive grid balancing.

By accurately predicting active power output before it is generated, energy stakeholders can optimise dispatch schedules, reduce curtailment, and maximise renewable penetration.
